Objectives & Methodology

General Objective

Create a cost-effective, user-friendly smart bracelet that continuously monitors the health of elderly individuals, provides fall detection, and sends instant alerts to caregivers or family members in the event of an emergency.

Specific Objectives

  • Health Monitoring: Develop sensors that accurately track vital health signs such as heart rate, temperature, and blood pressure, delivering real-time data.
  • Fall Detection: Integrate an accelerometer and develop algorithms to identify falls and send automatic alerts.
  • User-Centered Design: Create an intuitive interface on both the bracelet and the companion app for ease of use.
  • Affordable and Accessible: Use affordable and accessible components for feasibility in mass production.

Methodology

  • Research & Ideation: Understand the needs of elderly individuals and caregivers, and review current technologies.
  • Design & Prototyping: Move from concept to a functional prototype, meeting monitoring and design goals.
  • Testing & Iteration: Conduct real-world testing, refining both device and app based on feedback.
  • Deployment & Scaling: Roll out a reliable product with options for future upgrades based on user feedback.

Expected Outcomes

A fully functional and tested smart bracelet ready for real-world use, helping elderly individuals live independently with confidence and providing peace of mind to their families.
