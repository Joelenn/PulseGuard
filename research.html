Research & Development

Research and Conceptualization

The foundation of this project lies in understanding the specific health risks faced by the elderly population and leveraging technology to mitigate them. Through research, we identified the most critical parameters for monitoring: heart rate, blood pressure, temperature, and sudden falls. Our goal was to create a low-cost solution that doesn’t compromise on functionality or ease of use.

Selecting the Right Technology

  • Heart Rate & SpO2 Sensor (MAX30100/02): Provides continuous monitoring of the heart rate and oxygen saturation levels.
  • Accelerometer (MPU6050): Detects sudden movements or falls by measuring changes in orientation.
  • Temperature Sensor (DS18B20): Monitors body temperature, an important indicator of fever or hypothermia.
  • Arduino/ESP32 Microcontroller: The “brain” of the device, processing the data and ensuring seamless communication with the mobile app.
  • Bluetooth Module (HC-05): Allows real-time data transmission to the mobile app, enabling caregivers to receive alerts immediately.

Testing & Prototyping

Our prototype went through rigorous testing phases to refine accuracy, minimize errors, and ensure ease of use. We are currently in the phase of optimizing battery life while maintaining high-quality performance.

Future Development

We plan to enhance features like integration with voice assistants (e.g., Google Assistant, Alexa) for ease of use and explore more advanced health metrics such as ECG for heart health.
